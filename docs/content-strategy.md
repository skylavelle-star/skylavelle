# Content Strategy - Lavelle

## Target audience

### Primary: Senior project managers and program managers

- 10 to 25+ years of delivery experience
- Currently managing complex enterprise technology programs
- Working in government, financial services, utilities, higher education, or professional services
- Dealing with failing programs, difficult stakeholders, procurement challenges, or executive reporting
- Looking for practical tools and guidance, not theory or generic frameworks

### Secondary: Technology leaders and executives

- CTOs, CIOs, technology directors
- Responsible for delivery programs they do not directly manage
- Need to understand program health, make governance decisions, or commission advisory support
- May be the buyer for advisory services or the consumer of templates for their team

### Tertiary: PMO leads and delivery managers

- Establishing standards for their organisation's delivery practice
- Looking for templates and frameworks they can adapt
- Potential buyers for template packs

---

## Positioning

Lavelle is the professional resource for senior practitioners dealing with the hardest parts of enterprise technology delivery.

Not generic project management. Not entry-level content. Not startup culture or Agile evangelism.

Senior, direct, commercially grounded. Australian English. Practical outcomes.

The positioning is built around three things that experienced practitioners actually struggle with:
1. Recovering programs that have gone wrong
2. Navigating the governance and procurement requirements of complex enterprise environments
3. Communicating with executives and boards in ways that build confidence rather than erode it

## Passive income model

### Revenue streams

**Tier 1 - Free (email capture)**
- Checklists distributed via Beehiiv newsletter subscription
- Builds the list, demonstrates value, drives product ladder ascent

**Tier 2 - Low-cost templates ($197-$297 AUD)**
- Self-service template packs for specific delivery challenges
- No recurring revenue, but high margin and low support overhead
- Target: 10-20 sales per month per product at $197-$297

**Tier 3 - Advisory (day rate + project basis)**
- Highest revenue, lowest volume
- Newsletter and articles drive inbound leads
- Target: 2-4 concurrent advisory clients

### Product ladder

1. Free newsletter subscription (entry point)
2. Free checklists (email capture, value demonstration)
3. Template packs at $197-$297 (first purchase, trust established)
4. Advisory engagement inquiry (highest value conversion)

## Newsletter strategy

### Publication: The Project Recovery Brief

**Frequency:** Weekly, published Monday mornings

**Format:** Single topic, 600-900 words. One specific challenge, one direct response. No introduction, no summary, no padding.

**Voice:** Experienced colleague, not consultant. Direct. No hedging. Occasionally opinionated.

**Growth strategy:**
- Organic search via articles (main driver)
- Free resource gating - checklist downloads require subscription
- Template product post-purchase upsell to newsletter

**Monetisation:**
- Primary: drives advisory inquiries and template sales
- Secondary: potential for sponsored issues once list reaches 2,000+ engaged subscribers
- No affiliate marketing

**Content themes:**
- Project recovery tactics
- Stakeholder and executive communication
- Procurement and governance
- Business case development
- Organisational dynamics in enterprise delivery
- Career and practice development for senior PMs

### SEO approach

- One high-quality article per primary keyword cluster
- Articles 1,500-3,000 words with genuine depth
- No keyword stuffing - write for the practitioner, not the search engine
- Internal linking between related articles and relevant template pages
- Schema markup for articles (Article type, BreadcrumbList)
- Sitemap submitted to Google Search Console
